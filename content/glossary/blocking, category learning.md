When structuring the order categories are taught, blocking is focusing on categories in serial, as opposed to rotating them ([[interleaving, category learning|interleaving]]), during a learning session. When studying chess tactics this could look like focusing on knight forks, then queen forks, then rook forks and then ending the learning session. Compared to interleaving which would spend less time on each category and then circle back around to knight forks.[^1].

[^1]: As to whether or not either is a good idea, see [[blocked vs interleaving]] and walk away with more questions.
