Interleaving teaches learning concepts A, B, C in the order of ABCABC, whereas blocking puts them in the order AABBCC.

The benefits of interleaving are clear from the research. While some studies show that high within-category variability (e.g. identify art eras) might benefit from blocking, low within-category variability (e.g. identifying artists) benefit from [[interleaving, category learning]].[^1] Others, such as MacKendrick (2015), show no advantage of blocking. As a caveat, in this last study the condition of simultaneous "low-between similarity" and "low-within similarity" categories suffered a floor effect, meaning the learner's scores were too low to differentiate an effect.

[^1]: Firth, Jonathan, et al. “A Systematic Review of Interleaving as a Concept Learning Strategy.” _Revista De Educacion_, 2021, [https://doi.org/10.1002/REV3.3266](https://doi.org/10.1002/REV3.3266).
