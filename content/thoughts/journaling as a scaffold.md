Nückles, Matthias, et al (2020) explored journal writing activities through the lens of self-regulated learning, reasoning and optimizing within that framework. They connected journal writing to cognitive load optimization and also framed it as a self-regulated learning event. They refer to this as the "self-regulation-view of writing-to-learn".[^1]

This idea of linking journal writing to a larger system and then with it enabling that system, might be worth exploring in other topics. The design of a journal writing activity, such as which prompts to provide, should reflect the goals of that system. The line between what is inherent in the activity and what can and can't be introduced via task design are important questions in such an endeavor.

There exists single player role playing games that are driven by journal writing. That are also products that take this idea and go one step further by using the structure of journal writing to add fantasy elements to every day chores and tasks.

[^1]: Nückles, Matthias, et al. “The Self-Regulation-View in Writing-to-Learn: Using Journal Writing to Optimize Cognitive Load in Self-Regulated Learning.” _Educational Psychology Review_, 2020, [https://doi.org/10.1007/S10648-020-09541-1](https://doi.org/10.1007/S10648-020-09541-1).
